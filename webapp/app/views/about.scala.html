Frequently Asked Questions

If your question is not answered, feel free to use the feedback link at the bottom of the page.

Why does NewsSnips exist?

There is mounting evidence that the volume of information being consumed by the average user is increasing. The growth in content consumption aids doomscrooling.

The result is a population that is increasingly experiencing information overload as argued by The Shallows, Amusing Ourselves to Death and Irresistible.

We want to counter these effects by making news and social media more digestible so users can stay informed with the help of technology without feeling like the world is coming to an end.

Isn't this harder than just reading the news/social media?

Somewhat.

Mood assignment gives you the power to focus on the positives while also giving your a prospective on the scale of the negativity on a trending topic.

We believe you should exercise your right to control where you spend your attention. Therefore, we consider being able to choose the topics and mood a privilege all users should have in the modern information age to combat sensationalism.

How do you handle bias?

NewsSnips ingests feeds by most major news sources such as CNN, Fox news and the WSJ. We also leverage social media platforms like Reddit. More sources coming soon to provide a comprehensive view of the news landscape. If you’d like to suggest a source, please use the feedback option.

Some numbers don't add up!

Occasionally, the system is unable to determine if a content is strictly positive or negative. In that case, the statement is added to both mood categories for completeness.

What are topic types?

For example, the system attempts to distinguish between Apple the company and the fruit using separate topic types. Hovering the mouse over the topic type will provide a description of the type.

What are sources?

The links visible when you click the "Sources" button above the topics represent the source of content.
