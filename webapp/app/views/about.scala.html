Frequently Asked Questions

If your question is not answered, feel free to use the feedback link at the bottom of the page.

Why does this site exist?

There is mounting evidence that the volume of information being consumed by the average user is increasing. The growth in content consumption aids doomscrooling.

The result is a population that is increasingly exepriencing information overload as argued by The Shallows, Amusing Ourselves to Death and Irresistible.

We want to counter these effects by making news and information media in general more digestable so consumers of information can stay informed with the help of modern data ingestion technology.

Why is the total mentions count less than positive + negative mentions?

Occasionally, the system is unable to determine if a statement is strictly positive/negative. In that case, the statement is added to both sentient categories for completeness.

How do you handle bias?

Although it is easy to determine if there is a large positive/negative reporting skew on certain topics on newssnips, the labeling of a specific mention largely depends on the data used to train the open source ML model. A factor outside our control as users of those published models. In the future, we hope to be able to train our own models to reduce implicit labeling bias much as possible.

What are topic types?

For example, the system attempts to distinguish between Apple the company and the fruit using seperate topic types. Hovering the mouse over the topic type will provide a description of the type.

What are sources?

The links visible when you click the "sources" button above the topics represent the RSS feed used to gather the raw data.
